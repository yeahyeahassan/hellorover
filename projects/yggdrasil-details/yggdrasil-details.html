YGGDRASIL


Chassis

Made out of Carbon-fiber, made YGGDRASIL rigid and stable yet lightweight to withstand extreme conditions.

Motors

High Torque BLDC Motor Driven Actuators with weight distribution techniques made the steering mechanism of YGGDRASIL better then before.

Wheels

3D Printed Custom Design wheels enables YGGDRASIL to manuever over extreme terrains.

Primary Manipulator Arm

Handles payload upto 15 kg and can perform precision tasks. Operated by burshless motor driven cycloidal actuators with a parallel sliding end-effector.

Steering Mechanism

Brushless Geared Motors powers YGGDRASIL to drive through extreme challenges.

Rover Communication Link

Long Range 915Mhz Communication System enables telemetry and remote control over 2.5 KM.

Video Transmission Link

High Power 5.8 Ghz Video Transmission Link allows YGGDRASIL to transmit encrypted low-latency uninturrupted video feed to over 2.5 KM.

The tree of life


YGGDRASIL is our 3rd Generation Mars Rover Prototype. The upgraded carbon fiber chassis, paired with newly optimized 3D-printed wheels, delivers optimal suspension and traction, enabling YGGDRASIL to navigate demanding terrain with precision and reliability.


Autonomous Sub-system


The rover navigates autonomously using an advanced system that clears obstacles, replans paths, and recovers from challenges. It combines data from a depth camera, GPS, and motion sensors for precise movement. With LiDAR and cameras, it detects and avoids obstacles in real time, including potholes. A smart object detection system identifies items from up to 16 meters away, while another system tracks special markers for navigation. A dashboard at the base station displays key mission updates, and all processing runs on a powerful Jetson AGX Orin, ensuring smooth and reliable performance in tough environments.

Science Sub-system


The system analyzes soil and atmospheric conditions to identify habitable sites. A Raman spectrometer selects the top two locations. The wet lab detects key biomolecules, while the science wrist enables contamination-free sampling. Soil sensors assess habitability, and a regolith collector gathers samples. A panorama camera surveys terrain, with all data integrated into a central dashboard for real-time monitoring.
